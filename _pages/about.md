I am an Electrical Engineer with a focus on embedded systems, robotics, and software architecture, with extensive industry experience in defense and aerospace. Over the past decade, I’ve contributed to high-speed loitering UAS solutions, distributed real-time control systems, and sensor-integrated platforms. My work spans FPGA design, embedded Linux, vision-based guidance, and autonomous control systems. I have also led cross-functional teams, implemented modular software architectures, and developed automated testing frameworks and CI/CD pipelines to ensure reliable, field-ready systems.

In addition to my industry experience, I have a strong academic background in electrical engineering, including a Master’s thesis on neural network training that explored entropy and periodicity for autonomous systems applications. I have conducted research in dynamical systems, chaos theory, and real-time machine learning for embedded platforms. These experiences have allowed me to combine theoretical rigor with applied engineering, enabling the design of innovative solutions for complex control and autonomy problems.

Outside of work, I pursue hands-on projects in robotics, autonomous vehicles, and embedded machine learning. Examples include autonomous parafoil recovery systems, open-source chaotic oscillator shields, and smart sensor networks. I enjoy exploring how emerging technologies can be applied to real-world systems, and I am passionate about bridging the gap between hardware and software to create practical, high-impact engineering solutions.
